## Interpretation

v23 is a local refinement around the surviving v22 selector family. The key question is whether any nearby task-grad weight or a small query residual beats `VT-half` once rerun under matched `L`, `XL`, `T2a-XL`, and fresh-seed `T1-R` budgets.

The answer is no. `VT-0.5+Q0.25` does not survive as a better default. Its only remaining edge is `T2a-XL` last-checkpoint mean, while `VT-0.5` is better on `Core-XL` last-checkpoint mean and `Core-XL` dense mean. `T1-XL` is a tie, `T2a-XL` dense mean is a tie, and `T1-R` fresh reruns are also a tie on dense and last-checkpoint means. That makes the residual query term look like a fragile stress-only spike rather than a robust upgrade.

The practical v23 recommendation is therefore to keep `VT-0.5` as the selector default within this local neighborhood. The additional task-grad weights (`VT-0.25`, `VT-0.75`) and the small-query residual hybrid did not beat it once the comparison was rerun under longer matched budgets.
